Instructions for Setting Up
NotebookLMs for Qualitative Research

Rodrigo Cetina-Presuel — UPF Barcelona School of Management

Below you will find simple instructions to “clone” my NotebookLMs for your personal use. Once you have set them up you will be able to:

  1. Use the NotebookLMs to explore resources about how to conduct qualitative research and to plan and chart things such as coherence matrixes, rubrics for analyzing (public) documents, templates for interviews, consent forms, charts for testing the coherence and pertinence of your coding sheets, themes, etc. Play around, you’ll see!
  2. For actual research, what I recommend is that you load the NotebookLM(s) to Google Gemini or Claude and work with your documents or transcripts there. You may also upload your own charts, rubrics, matrixes, etc., and have the GPTs code, extract passages from the transcripts and match them to your themes or subthemes, etc. Consider this experimental — this is not Atlas.TI or Dedoose, but maybe we are on to something!
  3. This is released open source; license (GNU AGPL 3.0) is on the readme file. Please, just kindly quote me if you use it.
  4. Need any help or want to discuss how you are using this? Reach out at rodrigo.cetina@bsm.upf.edu.
  5. Always verify your work: AI can make mistakes, hallucinate, and results should always be approached with a critical eye.

Qualitative Methods for the Study of Public Policy

This repository contains general resources on qualitative methodologies for public policy. It covers everything from an introduction to the field through sampling strategies, types of methodologies, and specifics of each approach, as well as information on coding and more. It includes five books plus additional resources.

Instructions

  1. Follow the Dropbox link to download all the files. You can also download them here.
  2. Open NotebookLM — you can click here to access it — and sign in.
  3. In NotebookLM, create a new Notebook named QUALITATIVE METHODOLOGIES AND PUBLIC POLICY. WARNING: DO NOT mix these files with those from any other folder.
  4. Add all the required files (PDFs) to the NotebookLM. Make sure they are all correctly loaded.
  5. Start using NotebookLM thoughtfully and responsibly.
